2. What characterizes an athlete with a high score of factor 1 and of factor 2? Are there big differences between the interpretation of the unrotated and the rotated factors?

A high value of rotated factor 1 means that the running times in hurdles, 200 meters and 800 meters are very large, i.e. the athlete is slow, combined with low values of highjump, longjump and shot, again an indication of a not so good athlete. A high value of rotated factor 2 corresponds to high values in javelin and shot (and to a lesser extent low 200 meters running time long longjumps), i.e. we have an athlete not bad at running and jumping who is very good in javelin and shot. The major difference between the rotated and unrotated analysis is a change of sign. This is also confirmed by the factor patterns depicted below.
